The Digital Divide (SDG 10)

Our focus is on the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 10: "Reduced Inequalities"[cite: 2]. The digital divide refers to the unequal access to information and communication technologies[cite: 2]. According to recent data, only 18.6% of schools in Sri Lanka had internet access for educational purposes in 2019[cite: 2]. By providing a reliable Wi-Fi connection to Eswaththa Pandukabhaya Maha Vidyalaya in Awissawella, we aim to ensure that students have equal access to online learning sources[cite: 2].

Partners & Sponsors

In collaboration with Group O (Project 50:50), this project takes a comprehensive approach[cite: 2]. While Project 50:50 focuses on SDG 5 (Gender Equality) and provides a computer and a table, ONE HUMANITY (Group G) focuses on SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities) by supplying a chair and a Wi-Fi router[cite: 2]. Together, these contributions will establish a functional digital learning station[cite: 2].
