School Wi-Fi Initiative

Vision: One Humanity, One Connection, Equal Opportunity[cite: 1].

Mission

To reduce digital inequality by providing rural classrooms with the routers, connectivity, and digital literacy skills needed to thrive in a modern world[cite: 1].

The Problem

  • The Digital Divide in Education: Students in economically disadvantaged schools in Sri Lanka have limited access to reliable internet, restricting their ability to use online learning platforms, conduct research, and develop digital literacy skills[cite: 1].
  • Inequality of Opportunity: This lack of connectivity creates unequal learning opportunities compared to students in better-resourced urban schools[cite: 1].
  • Sri Lankan Context: Only 18.6% of schools in Sri Lanka had internet access for education in 2019, with over 80% of schools in under-resourced areas still lacking reliable connectivity[cite: 1].

The Solution

We are establishing a Digital Learning Station at Eswaththa Pandukabhaya Maha Vidyalaya in Awissawella[cite: 1]. This project is a collaboration with Group O (Project 50:50); while they provide the computer and table, One Humanity provides the Wi-Fi router and a chair[cite: 1]. We also conduct a "Digital Literacy Day" to train students on safe and productive internet use[cite: 1].

Project Timeline

Project Impact

Quantitative: Over 100 students gain access to digital resources, with online learning activities anticipated to increase by over 60%[cite: 1].

Qualitative: This initiative bridges the digital divide, improves digital literacy, and creates inclusive education opportunities, directly supporting SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities)[cite: 1].
