Vegetable Seed Distribution Project

Organized By: 25.2 Batch[cite: 3]

Addressing Inequality at its Roots

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 10 (SDG 10) reminds us that no individual should be left behind due to their economic, social, or geographic circumstances[cite: 3]. Embracing this vision, Batch 25.2 has launched this community-driven initiative to address inequality at its roots[cite: 3]. We identified that a significant inequality exists in access to nutritious food—many families cannot afford fresh vegetables or lack the knowledge to grow their own[cite: 3]. This project directly tackles that gap[cite: 3].

Empowering Families

The primary objective of this project is to empower 20 underprivileged and low-income families by providing them with high-quality vegetable seeds, along with essential technical guidance delivered through a certified Agricultural Facilitator[cite: 3].

By partnering with an Agricultural Facilitator, we ensure that each beneficiary family receives not just seeds, but also hands-on training in sustainable home gardening practices – from soil preparation to pest management and harvesting[cite: 3]. This holistic approach guarantees that families can successfully cultivate their own gardens, reducing their dependency on costly market produce[cite: 3].

Why SDG 10?

This project is a direct response to SDG 10.2 – promoting the social and economic inclusion of all[cite: 3]. By providing equal access to the resources and knowledge needed for food production, we are reducing the inequality gap between well-off families and those struggling with food insecurity[cite: 3]. Additionally, we contribute to SDG 10.3 by ensuring equal opportunities for better nutrition and health outcomes, regardless of a family's financial background[cite: 3].

Project Highlights

  • Beneficiaries: 20 low-income families[cite: 3].
  • Partner: Certified Agricultural Facilitator[cite: 3].
  • Key Activities: Seed distribution, hands-on training, technical support[cite: 3].
  • SDG Alignment: SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities)[cite: 3].
  • Organized By: 25.2 Batch[cite: 3].
